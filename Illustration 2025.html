Welcome to illustration Art of Design

To negotiate a complex design contract it is important to establish a dialogue between the designer and the client. Here, the designer has to prepare a design brief to guide him or her in the communication process while the client on the other hand participate and finally reach an agreement upon understanding the design brief. Discussions will also involve design options, cost and quotations and optional printing processes and specifications and deadlines for project submission.

Common Terminologies

-Communication Design is what makes the difference between good and bad graphic communications. To design is to make something work, to fit the particular need for which it is created. A successful design is the one that suits the client’s requirements and its attractive (aesthetic) or functional (workable). Design options These are design tools that may require a range of manual equipment or hardware and design software applications. Normally a designer has to decide on the compatibility of both hardware and software required to produce design work to standard meeting printing requirements. Cost This is in real life project where hourly rates, material costs and any other factor contributing to job costs are identified and projected in the initial planning stages. Quotation A document provided by the supplier containing information about an item and cost before purchasing. Design Process Involves design concept development to final product output following a client brief. A process that guides the designer to solve client need. Steps involved in generating ideas in response to the design brief.
